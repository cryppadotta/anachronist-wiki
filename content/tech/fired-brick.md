Fired Brick can create ordinary workshop hazards through heat, sharp edges, dust, spoilage, lifting, caustic residues, splinters, or failed loads depending on the material. ## Procedure

1. Select one small candidate source.
2. Remove obvious dirt, damage, rot, or unsuitable portions.
3. Prepare a trial batch or trial part.
4. Use the trial on scrap or low-value work.
5. Record the source, preparation, and observed result.
6. Keep one successful sample and one failed sample for comparison.

## Verification and quality control

A useful fired brick result repeats from the same source and fails visibly before it endangers important work. Check fit, strength, wear, shrinkage, contamination, cracking, loosening, alignment, or record clarity as appropriate. Compare new batches against retained references.
