## Field briefing

Timber seasoning turns fresh [[timber|timber]] into more predictable stock. It is a storage and patience technology as much as a wood technology.

## What you are trying to make

Create wood that changes shape before it becomes a finished part, not after it is built into a wheel, frame, door, or tool handle.

## Minimum viable version

Split or rough-shaped pieces are stored off the ground under cover with air around them and are checked before use.

## Better versions

Better versions sort by species, size, date, intended use, and observed movement. Critical parts are rough shaped first, rested, and then finished.

## Prerequisite tree

- [[timber|Timber]] selection.
- [[counting|Counting]] and [[writing|writing]] for stock age and source.
- [[woodworking|Woodworking]] skills for reading grain and movement.

## Materials and sourcing

The material is green or damp wood from felling, salvage, drift, or trade. Recognition begins with weight, smell, end checking, surface dampness, and whether shavings feel wet or dry.

Acquisition is tied to storage: taking more timber than can be kept dry wastes labor. Preparation includes rough trimming, removing trapped bark where pests or rot are a problem, spacing pieces, weighting or stacking them straight, and protecting them from rain while allowing airflow. Substitutes include using green wood for parts that can shrink safely, using naturally dry deadwood when sound, or choosing designs that tolerate movement. Damp Britain-like climates make covered airflow especially important.

## Tools and workshop requirements

Tools include racks, spacers, cord, wedges, marking tools, and a roofed or sheltered place.

## Procedure

1. Sort timber by job and size.
2. Rough shape oversized pieces.
3. Mark source and date.
4. Store off the ground with airflow.
5. Watch for twist, cracks, insects, and rot.
6. Reserve the most stable pieces for precision parts.

## Verification and quality control

Compare weight, sound, visible movement, and trial fitting over time. A test piece can be planed flat, rested, and checked again. Stock that keeps changing belongs in lower precision work.
